A Higher Farewell

Cremation is no longer the alternative — it's becoming the standard. With nearly two-thirds of Americans now choosing cremation, millions of families face the same question: What do we do with the ashes?

ASH International answers that question with something extraordinary. We organize aviation-based ash-dispersal ceremonies that transform a final goodbye into a breathtaking, spiritual celebration of life.

From skydivers tracing an owl formation at 12,000 feet, to hot air balloons drifting over the landscapes your loved one cherished, to international expeditions releasing ashes over Easter Island, Machu Picchu, the Galápagos, and beyond — ASH creates moments that families remember forever.

As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, ASH is mission-driven, not profit-driven. Every ceremony is handled with reverence, professionalism, and the unique personal touch that honors each individual life.

Every service is conducted with the highest safety standards, reverence, and artistry. Choose the experience that honors your loved one best.

The 7-Step Continuous Loop

Each of ASH's three organizations — and ASH itself — operates on a rigorous 7-step improvement cycle mapped to the triangle's edges.

1
Define

Map suppliers, inputs, activities, outputs, customers, and boundaries to clearly identify the process scope.

2
Uncover

Spot 50-75 improvement areas during mapping, focusing on user-reported issues and their costs.

3
Measure

Create metrics to assess effectiveness, efficiency, responsibility, and customer feedback.

4
Analyze

Evaluate task value, time, waste, and customer relationships to pinpoint root causes.

5
Act

Implement fixes, solving ~50% of issues (like poor definition) within 90 days.

6
Control

Add controls — defined value, metrics, feedback loops, and corrective mechanisms for stability.

7
Monitor

Track performance monthly, replacing resolved issues with new challenges. The loop restarts.

A → H (Steps 1-3)  ·  H → S (Steps 4-5)  ·  S → A (Steps 6-7)  ·  ∞ Loop

The Documemorial™

Documentary + Memorial. We capture the full story of a life and broadcast the ceremony to the world.

A Life Story, Beautifully Told

The Documemorial is a professionally produced short documentary that weaves together the story of the person being honored — their passions, accomplishments, relationships, and the places that mattered most to them.

Combined with full live-stream coverage of the ash dispersal ceremony, the Documemorial creates a rich, participatory experience that connects loved ones around the world in real time.

Afterwards, families receive a high-quality edited film, the ceremonial dispersal vessel as a keepsake, and a permanent digital memorial page accessible to family and friends.

The ASH Reactor

A self-replicating organizational model — 7 divisions, 3 departments — where each part mirrors the whole. The triangle within the triangle within the triangle.

The "Reactor" is Bill Liebhart's fractal blueprint for ASH. The master triangle — A (App), S (Special Events), H (Hero/Guild) — contains within it smaller triangles that replicate the same structure. Each division runs the same 7-step continuous improvement loop. Each department contains its own A, S, and H. It's recursive by design — scalable, self-correcting, and endlessly replicating.

The Dispersal Vessel

A handcrafted, high-quality container that becomes a cherished memento of the ceremony.

Proprietary Design, Personal Meaning

The ASH dispersal vessel is engineered for reliable, elegant in-flight release while serving as a beautiful keepsake afterwards. Each vessel can be crafted from materials of personal significance — rare wood, stone, recycled metals, or custom materials chosen by the family.

After the ceremony, the vessel is returned to the family as a permanent memento — engraved with the date, location, coordinates, and a personal message. It becomes a tangible connection to the extraordinary moment when their loved one took flight one final time.

  • Precision-engineered dispersal mechanism (patent pending)
  • Custom materials: exotic wood, crystalline stone, artisan metals
  • Engraved with GPS coordinates, date, and personal inscription
  • Aerodynamically designed for all flight platforms
  • Returned to family as a memorial keepsake
  • Optional: matching miniature vessel for family members

The ASH Guild

An elite network of skydivers, pilots, and aviators — trained, certified, and united by a sacred mission.

Skydiver Requirements

USPA B-license minimum (C or D preferred). 200+ jumps. Formation flying experience. Must complete ASH dispersal certification program.

Pilot Requirements

Current FAA certification (PPL minimum, CPL preferred). 500+ hours PIC. Must hold current medical certificate and complete ASH mission protocols.

Hang Glider / Paraglider

USHPA Advanced (P4/H4) rating. Tandem instructor certification. 300+ flight hours. Insurance and site-specific approvals.

BASE Jumpers

500+ BASE jumps. Wingsuit experience preferred. Must demonstrate consistent safety record and complete ASH risk management training.

Balloon Pilots

FAA lighter-than-air certificate. Commercial balloon pilot license. 200+ flight hours. Must complete ASH passenger-family protocol training.

Dispersal Certification

All Guild members must complete the proprietary ASH Dispersal Device training — a 2-day intensive covering device operation, formation protocols, and ceremonial procedures.
